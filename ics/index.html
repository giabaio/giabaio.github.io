REF2029

Key changes (from REF2029 website)

  • The three elements familiar from REF 2014 and REF 2021 have been renamed, their content adjusted and their weightings rebalanced to reflect this:
    • People, culture and environment (25% weighting) replaces the environment element and will be expanded to include research culture
    • Contribution to knowledge and understanding (50% weighting) replaces the outputs element. It will largely be based on assessment of research outputs, but will also include evidence of broader contributions to the advancement of the discipline
    • Engagement and impact (25% weighting) replaces the impact element. It is similar to the impact element of REF 2014 and will consist of impact case studies and an accompanying statement
  • In the second stage of the consultation (December 2023):

The minimum number of Impact Case Studies that an institution can submit per disciplinary submission will be reduced to one, and the removal of the 2* quality threshold is confirmed. Proposals related to the revision of the thresholds for the number of Impact Case Studies that are required to be submitted, and the weighting of the structured statement will be considered further.

Impact Case Studies (ICSs)

We don’t yet have a new template, so things may slightly change… BUT: in 2021

1. Summary of the impact (max 100 words)

  • “Researchers at UCL Department of Statistical Science did …”
  • “Our work demonstrated potential to achieve …”
  • “This generated estimated income of £…”

2. Underpinning research (max 500 words)

  • “Prof/Dr X research focuses on …”
  • “There’s a problem with how people do this sort of things, so we fixed it” (novel approach)
  • “The research underpinning this study has been conducted by the XXX group, including …”

3. References to research (max 6 references)

  • Ideally top journals (duh!…)
  • NB: That’s discipline/application specific! Doesn’t have to be JASA/Biometrika, necessarily…

4. Details of impact (max 750 words)

  • Impact for who? (Funder/Industrial partner, Government, …)
  • Can it be monetised?
  • Did it change policy? If so, how many people where affected?
  • Was it used locally? Internationally?

5. Sources to corroborate the impact (max 10 references)

  • That’s probably the hardest to find — would require some time for preparation!
  • This could be letter of endorsement from your indutrial partners, minutes of committees, published guidelines or financial records, etc …

Cost-effectiveness of HPV vaccination

Motivation

  • Originally, vaccination only targeted females and typically “too late” (in Italy, vaccination age: 24yo)
  • Main objective of research: evaluate cost-effectiveness of “universal” vaccination (females + males, 12-18 yo)

Typical modelling

  1. Complex mathematical model for transmission dynamics (based on ODEs)
  2. (ridiculously!) Basic economic model — plug-in estimates + deterministic sensitivity analysis

Novel approach

  • Compromise on time resolution (discretise transmission dynamics)
  • Can afford full modelling of epidemiologic and economic components in a single “Markov model” (statisticians would call that a “multistate transition model”)
  • Can afford a full structure with over 50 states, accounting for many potential side effects simultaneously

Demonstrable impact

Change in national guidelines

  • Also thanks to our work, the Italian Government modified the national guidelines and adopted universal vaccination for 12-18yo cohorts
  • Also thanks to our work, other countries followed suit, including UK and Canada

Economic benefits

  • Manufacturer of new vaccine increased their sales
  • Estimated income of around €22m

Methodological spillovers

  • Arguments from our methodology/papers used in another relevant case (Meningococcal B vaccine)
    • Epi model was OK, but economic model was awful
    • Vaccine refused reimbursement by JVCI (UK body responsible for advice on vaccines)
    • Decision later modified, enabling the annual protection of 800,000 babies (at a cost of £16m)
